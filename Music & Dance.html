4. thomo

Thomo is a musical instrument that has a calabash attached to a bow made of mountain bamboo near the bottom end and string made of horse hair.

5. lekolulo

6. lesiba

7 .setolotolo

Setolotolo is a traditional Basotho musical instrument, often referred to as a mouth bow. It’s a simple yet deeply expressive instrument that reflects the ingenuity and musical soul of the Basotho people.

8. sekupu

Sekupu is a traditional Basotho musical instrument—specifically, a drum made from cowhide stretched over a 20-litre tin. It plays a central role in litolobonya, a ceremonial song and dance tradition performed exclusively by Basotho women and girls

9. sekebeku sa lehlaka

Sekebeku sa lehlaka is a traditional Basotho musical instrument made from lehlaka, which means reed or bamboo. It’s a variation of the sekebeku sa ts’epe (metal jaw harp), but crafted from natural plant material instead of metal—giving it a softer, more earthy resonance.

10. moropa

Moropa is the Sesotho word for drum, and it holds a powerful place in Basotho traditional music and culture.

11. sekebeku sa ts'epe

The sekebeku sa ts’epe is a traditional Basotho musical instrument, also known as a jaw harp. It’s a small but powerful piece of cultural heritage that produces a twangy, rhythmic sound when played.

12. mamokhorong

The mamokhorong is a fascinating traditional musical instrument of the Basotho people in Lesotho. It’s often described as a one-string violin, though its construction and sound are quite unique to the region

13. litolobonya

litolobonya is a dance performed by young Basotho women, expresing their femininity, grace, and strength. it is an important rite of passage for young girls.

14. Tsilo

This dance is performed in circular formations and symbolizes unity and love among the Basotho community.

15. mokhibo

mokhibo embobies the stotytelling tradition of the Basotho people. it portrays their history,legends,and values through rhythmic movements and gesture.

16. setinkane

setinkane is a thumb piano adds mesmerizing tones to Basotho musical compositions.

17. lekolulo

The lekolulo , a flute-like instrument, is a key part of the Basotho music.

18. mohobelo

Mohobelo is a stride, slide and leap dance exclusively performed by Basotho men . Although it is terrritorial,in that the dance movements of the north differ.

19. khati

Khati is a traditional Basotho musical instrument, often associated with children’s play and informal music-making. It’s simple in design but rich in cultural value.

21. mokubung

Mokopung is a traditional Basotho musical instrument that reflects the creativity and resourcefulness of the Basotho people. While it's not as widely documented as instruments like the lesiba or mamokhorong, mokopung is part of the rich tapestry of indigenous sound-making tools used especially by herd boys and young musicians in rural Lesotho.

22. mokopu o omisitsoeng

Mokopu o omisitsoeng—which translates to “dried pumpkin”—is a traditional Basotho instrument that beautifully showcases the ingenuity of turning everyday natural materials into tools for music and storytelling

23. motjeko oa sehlopha

Motjeko oa sehlopha refers to group dance in Sesotho culture—an expressive and communal form of traditional Basotho performance. It’s not a musical instrument, but rather a cultural practice that blends music, movement, and storytelling.
